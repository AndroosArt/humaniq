# Human IQ Offer Ladder

## Purpose
Define the Human IQ offer ladder in plain English so routing, proposals, and onboarding all use the same commercial language.

## Naming choice for v1
Use this ladder:

1. Not a Fit Yet
2. Growth & Conversion Review
3. Growth & Conversion Audit
4. Strategy + Implementation Sprint
5. Ongoing Optimization Partner

## Why this naming
- `Growth & Conversion Review` is already the repo's front-door offer and should stay there.
- `Practical AI Blueprint` remains the downstream deliverable when it is the right output of a paid engagement.
- `Growth & Conversion Audit` is the clearest label for the paid strategy-first path that currently appears as `Strategy / audit only`.

## Offer definitions
### 1. Not a Fit Yet
What it is for:
- leads that are too early, too unclear, too price-constrained, or not commercially ready

What is included:
- brief response
- high-level explanation of why Human IQ is not recommending paid work right now
- optional suggestion to revisit later

When to use it:
- low urgency
- low budget readiness
- low maturity
- weak immediate fit

### 2. Growth & Conversion Review
What it is for:
- the front-door intake and diagnostic entry point

What is included:
- structured intake
- Human IQ review of the submission
- internal scoring and routing
- concise recommendation of the next best path

When to use it:
- as the starting point for nearly all new leads

Important boundary:
- this is not the full paid roadmap
- it should lead to a recommendation, not replace paid strategy

### 3. Growth & Conversion Audit
What it is for:
- clients who need strategic clarity before implementation

What is included:
- deeper diagnosis of lead flow, qualification, follow-up, conversion, and systems
- prioritized findings
- clear recommendation on what to fix first
- a paid deliverable that can become the client's Practical AI Blueprint

When to use it:
- the business is real, but the right implementation scope is not yet clear
- the client needs clarity and prioritization before a sprint

### 4. Strategy + Implementation Sprint
What it is for:
- clients with a clear, urgent, bounded improvement opportunity

What is included:
- defined sprint scope
- strategy translated into action
- implementation of the highest-value first phase
- practical handoff or stabilization at the end

When to use it:
- there is obvious revenue leakage
- the first implementation phase can be scoped responsibly now
- the client is ready to act

### 5. Ongoing Optimization Partner
What it is for:
- established businesses that need recurring improvement, not a one-time fix

What is included:
- ongoing optimization across the revenue conversion path
- regular review of lead flow, qualification, follow-up, pipeline handling, and practical systems
- continued refinement after the first fixes are in place

When to use it:
- the business already has demand
- Human IQ can create value month after month
- a sprint alone would be too narrow

## Pricing guidance
Keep pricing abstract in the repo until approved pricing logic is finalized.

Use:
- light / entry paid engagement
- defined sprint engagement
- ongoing partner engagement

Avoid publishing hard price points in this pack unless approved elsewhere.

## Practical AI Blueprint role
The Practical AI Blueprint is best treated as a downstream diagnostic or deliverable, not the front-door offer.

In practice:
- the Audit may produce the Practical AI Blueprint
- a Sprint may start from the Practical AI Blueprint
- an Ongoing Partner engagement may refine it over time
